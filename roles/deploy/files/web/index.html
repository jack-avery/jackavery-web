faq


1. if i'm paying you, why are you trying to release it under an open source license?
i advocate free and open source software.
when you pay me to create an application and i release it under GPL, i am allowing anyone with a mutual interest in the application you've asked me to create to use it. this makes these technologies more accessible for other people, and allows anyone to suggest changes to improve your application for free. including myself, if i see fit. having it public and open-source also means i get to show off my work with pride.
also worth noting i am happy to oblige if you want it to remain closed source, but this may make me less excited to work on it.

2. what can you actually do?
anything that involves technologies that i've used inside of my projects tab. i am also more than happy to learn new technologies required to complete your application, but understand that that means it will take longer to finish the contract. naturally, if a request includes a lot of technologies i'm unfamiliar with, i will make that clear to you during the discussion of our service agreement, and i'll try to come to a mutual agreement on fair pay.

3. what are some things you've already done under contract?
note that this list is not exhaustive as i have been asked to keep some confidential.
1. gpbl-bot, a 24/7 music playing discord bot for a rap community
2. boxes4foxes, a team fortress 2 community server network
3. SwishCast Central, a tf2 server for the MANN Magazine's podcast 'The SwishCast'
4. badwater bestwater, a 24/7 badwater tf2 server

4. my idea is a bit fuzzy or unfinished. can you work with that?
yes, but it will make discussion a bit longer. the service agreement makes clear the terms about Scope.
i will discuss with you and we will come to an agreement about what exactly your application should do, and any modifications to that "Scope" will need to be agreed to. this prevents any surprise changes to what you've asked me to create.

5. how does payment work? it's not exactly specified in the agreement above.
the exact method of payment will be discussed alongside the terms of our agreement and scope of project.
in most cases, i will send invoices to you over PayPal, and the frequency of these invoices will be made clear to you. if you aren't able to use PayPal, we will need to discuss another form of payment.

6. can you do x for FREE?
if i'm not being paid, the application being open-source is non-negotiable, and the idea you have better interest me enough or expose me to new technologies to make my time enjoyable and/or educational. in no way do i guarantee anyone will get anything for free, but shoot your shot anyways, and we'll talk!
